    Drum Sound – Massive Attack (SD3)

    Drum Sound at Massive Attack (SD3)

    Objective

    Drums dark, heavy, atmospheric, with soft kick and dry snare, typical of 90s Bristol trip-hop (Mezzanine, 100th Window).

    1. Choosing a kit in SD3.

    • Start with a Ludwig 70s kit or Gretsch (they are already dark and full).
    • Use few cymbals → dark HH, no bright crashes (or keep them but filtered).
    • Watch out for Overheads and Room: they are critical, more than close-mics.

    2. Routing in SD3

    • Kick → Direct Out (clean).
    • Snare → Direct Out.
    • HH and cymbals → turn it down a lot, keep it almost like "background noise".
    • Room → keep it high, it will be the glue of the kit.
    • Overheads → turn them down and filter out the highs, so they stay dark.

    3. Processing in Logic

    Kick - EQ: cut 250 Hz (-3 dB), boost to 60-70 Hz (+3 dB) → sub soft. - Slow compressor (30-40 ms attack) → let breathe. Snare - EQ: low cut 150 Hz, boost 200 Hz (+2 dB body), slight +3 dB at 2-3 kHz (snap). - Light saturation (Overdrive or Tape Delay trick). Hi-Hat / Cymbals - EQ: low pass 8-10 kHz → no brightness. - Short delay (1/16, low feedback) for movement. Room - Aggressive compressor (FET 1176 style, 8:1 ratio, fast attack, medium release) → characteristic "pump". - EQ: High cut 6 kHz for darkening.

    4. Bus Drums Processing

    • Parallel compression ("Super Punchy") → adds body and aggression.
    • Room + Overhead dark → mix it with the main bus.
    • Some dark ambient reverb (2-3 s, filtered).

    Final character

    • Deep, dark kick.
    • Dry snare, with character but not bright.
    • Compressed room that becomes part of the groove.
    • Cymbals almost hidden, only as texture.
    • Dark, hypnotic atmosphere → Massive Attack mood.
