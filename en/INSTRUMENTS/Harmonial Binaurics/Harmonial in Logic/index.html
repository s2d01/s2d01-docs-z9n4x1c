    Creative workflow with audio bounces

    1. Capture the moment

    • Let Harmonial Binauris run until something comes out that inspires you.
    • Make Bounce in Place right away.
    • Don't overthink it: accumulate lots of audio "clips," even short ones. It's like sampling your own synth.

    2. Chopping and re-arranging

    • Cut bounces into chunks (even 1/4 or 1/8 of a beat).
    • Rearrange the fragments to create new patterns, kind of like with a sampler.
    • If you want, use Strip Silence in Logic to have it help you automatically separate notes.

    3. Time-stretching & Flex

    • Enable Flex Time (Polyphonic or Slicing).
    • Stretch or tighten the fragments to match the groove of the song.
    • You can also get very interesting glitch and stutter effects.

    4. Layering

    • Duplicate the same clip and transpose it ±12 semitones.
    • Pan wide L/R → get spatial textures.
    • Or duplicate and process differently (one with reverb, the other with distortion).

    5. Granulation & glitches

    • Load bounces into a granular plugin (e.g. Alchemy which is already inside Logic).
    • Turn patterns into atmospheric pads, alien sounds or cinematic drones.

    6. Infinite resampling

    • When you have created an interesting layer, do Bounce in Place again.
    • Then repeat: cut, transpose, stretch →new still different material.
    • It's like building an archive of original samples just your own.

    -> Basically, Harmonial becomes your machine of surprises, and with audio bounces you turn it into a real personal sample library.
