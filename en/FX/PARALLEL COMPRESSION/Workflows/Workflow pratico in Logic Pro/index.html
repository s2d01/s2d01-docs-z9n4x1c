    Practical workflow in Logic Pro

    STEP 1 - Organization with Dedicated Bus.

    1. Create a "Drums Bus " → all Aux of SD3 in (already with Stack).

    2. Create a "Reverbs Ambient " → an Aux just for long/dark reverbs.

    3. Create a "Textures " → Bus where you send noises, pads, reverse, etc. **This way you immediately separate groove, space and ambience.

    STEP 2 - Bus "Ambient Reverbs"

    • Insert 2 different reverbs in series/parallel:

      • Short dark slate (1.2s) → high cut at 5 kHz, low cut at 200 Hz.

      • Long hall (3-4s) → dark filtered, perhaps with a slow modulator (chorus/phaser).

    • You can dose them with different Send: Kick and Snare a little, Hi-Hat and texture a lot.
      ** You immediately have the "layered space" typical of Eno.

    STEP 3 - "Textures" bus.

    • Here you send ambient recordings, synths, reverse FX.

    • Typical chain:

      • Band-pass EQ (300 Hz - 5 kHz).

      • Saturator (Saturn with a touch of "Warm Tape").

      • Shimmer reverb (pitch +12, 100% mix).
        ** This bus becomes the atmospheric spine behind the groove.

    STEP 4 - Drums Bus (Massive Attack feel).

    • Kick: EQ to free 60-70 Hz, slow compressor.

    • Snare: parallel compression (use Super Punchy).

    • Percussion: low-pass filter (no excess brightness), short ping-pong delay.
      ** So you get heavy but dark grooves without getting "techno".

    STEP 5 - Routing and control.

    • Send Drums Bus → a bit into the Ambient Reverbs (lots of hi-hat and snare, little kick).

    • Send Textures → a lot into the Ambient Reverbs (so they blend into the space).

    • Stereo Out → you already have the clean + dark + atmospheric sum.

    -> You end up with three main faders controlling everything:

    • Drums Bus (energy)

    • Reverbs Ambient (space)

    • Textures (atmosphere)

    How to use them together

    • Snare and HH → a little bit of Plate (for attack) + a touch of Hall (for tail)

    • Kick → very little Plate, almost no Hall

    • Texture and pad → mostly long Hall, little Plate

    • Environments and reverse FX → 100% Hall to create "soundscapes"
